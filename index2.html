The Web from 2007 to 2014
2007: Web Meets Mobile

1. 36% of American online adults consult Wikipedia.
2. January: iPhone announced
Apple announced the first iPhone, which featured a modified version of Safari. Many argue that the iPhone was the first mobile device to feature first class web browsing.
3. May: IE Web Developer Toolbar addon released
This addon featured on IE6 and IE7 allowed developers to Explore and Modify the DOM View HTML element attributes.
4. June: Safari 3 announced
Safari 3 was released for MAC OS, Windows and the iPhone.
5. October: Safari's Web Inspector debuts
Safari 3 introduced the Web Inspector, developer tools for Web developers.

2008: Power to the People

1. 2008 Presidential Elections
74% of Internet users (55% of the entire US population) say they went online during the presidential election to take part in or get news about the campaign.
2. January: HTML5 introduced
HTML5 was a response to the demand for a single markup language that could be written in either HTML or XHTML.
3. December: Google Chrome released
Based on webkit, the browser engine at the heart of Apple's Safari

2009: Microsoft is Back

1. March: Internet Explorer 8 released
IE8 featured support for some aspects of CSS3 and HTML5. This was a significant update for Microsoft and introduced “Compatibility Mode,” which was a more sophisticated version of doctype switching.
2. June: Microsoft Bing launches

2010: The Web Gets Social

1. March: Pinterest launches
2. October: Instagram launches
3. November: Wikileaks releases U.S. diplomatic cables
Wikileaks collaborates with major media organizations to release U.S. diplomatic cables

2011:

1. Planking/Tebowing:
The rise of image based social media trends of today's internet.

2. Friday by Rebecca Black
The first big, widespread, infamous, Youtube Bomb.

3. Minecraft:
The first and only successful indie game that made its impact not only in the gaming world, but in the internet and real world as well.

2012:

1. Gangnam Style:
Youtube's most watched video, and also the first ever video to surpass 1 Billion views.

2. Todd Akin
Todd Akin makes an insensitive comment about rape, goes viral, and instantly kills his political career.

3. KONY 2012
Independent documentarian releases documentary, comes under scrutiny for its authenticity while raising millions, then entire movement crashes when the documentarian has a public breakdown.

2013:

1. ‘Space Oddity’ Sung by Astronaut Chris Hadfield:
Hailed as the most expensive music video ever, also highlights humanity as a species and how much we need to work together.

2. Twerking
A phenomenon that raised concerns about the sexualization of people at a young age.

3. Harlem Shake
Flash mobs that worked together to compete for fame on Youtube.

2014:

1. Potato Salad Kickstarter:
People fund things now, games, movies, and even crazy projects: the potato salad kickstarter is the most interesting hail-mary kickstarter that ever was finished.

2. Ice Bucket Challenge:
Another video meme, but it included celebrities doing it for the fame/infamy. Eventually dissolved into a popularity contest, yet achieved its goals of raising awareness for ALS.

3. The Fappening
Major celebrity nude image leak. Raised concerns about hacking and privacy in the digital age.
